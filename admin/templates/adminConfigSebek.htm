Configure Sebek Variables
       
  Sebek is one of the primary tools used to capture the activity of an attacker. It's a client kernel module (or kernel patch) installed on the client honeypots. It then captures the attacker's actions on the honeypot, puts that information into special Sebek packets, then sends them over the network to be collected by the Honeywall. This option will allow you to configure how the Honeywall handles your sebek packets. You will be able to tell the firewall how to identify your sebek packets, and you will be able to tell it how to route them. If you don't know the answers to these questions, then ask the person who installed Sebek on the client honeypots.
